Project-1
Step Counter Using Tribielectric Energy Harvester (TEH) based Self powered sensor with ESP32

Abstract

Our project aims to develop an step-counting system or mechanism. The system comprises a sensor module consisting of a sensor, microcontroller, and battery, along with an associated Android application that displays step statistics to the user. The sensor utilized in this system is based on triboelectric energy harvesting (TEH) and operates as a self-powered sensor. This means that there is no need for an external power source, as the sensor converts mechanical energy into electrical energy, which powers its functions. When an individual takes a step, the sensor is pressed, and the mechanical energy is converted into electrical energy. This energy triggers the microcontroller to generate an interrupt signal, indicating a step has been taken. The microcontroller processes this interrupt and sends the corresponding signal to the application on a mobile phone, allowing the step count to be recorded and displayed.


Keywords:

Triboelectric Energy Harvesters (TEHs), ESP32- WROOM32, Step Counting, Internet of Things (IoT), Self-powered Sensors, Self-sustained sensing nodes, Battery-free IoT, Android Application, Qt Framework.
